Anne Thompson

Head of School - Design Innovation

Anne worked at numerous agencies before establishing her own design practice in London for eight years. Returning to New Zealand after 29 years living and working abroad, she fell into education while enquiring at Massey University about doing a PGDip in design.

Dan Watt

Head of School - Fashion & Sustainability

Dan completed his BA with a double major in English and Music at Victoria University and his Diploma of Journalism at AUT University. He worked as a freelance fashion designer in Wellington and Auckland for seven years.

Dave Ellwood

Head of School - Fine Arts

Dave completed his undergraduate studies at Auckland University’s Elam School of Fine Arts in 1996 and has a PGDip in Teaching from Auckland College of Education. He graduated with a Master of Fine Arts (1st class honours) from Elam in 2003.

Amanda Levey

Head of School - Creative Arts Therapies

Amanda gained her psychology degree from the University of Melbourne, and did extensive training in the USA in the Halprin Method, an integration of movement/dance, visual arts, performance techniques and therapeutic practices.

Jess Patterson

Programme Leader - Certificate Art & Design

Jess received her Bachelor of Fine Arts from Whitecliffe College in 2005. Prior to that she taught Expressive Arts in Papua New Guinea and had the privilege to work with the Yeraki people as they recorded their traditional arts practises culminating in a book called People and Plants.

Valerie Denny

Programme Leader - Fashion Tech

Growing up in a family of tailors and dressmakers, Valerie was encouraged from a young age to create her own clothes with a varying degree of success. This was the catalyst that inspired her journey into fashion education; first as a student and later, as a practitioner of fashion education.

Shirley Marks

Lecturer
